Worried about COVID? Let us help!

Here we bust some myths about COVID and provide helpful tips to beat the disease. Also included are a list of symptoms to help you identify COVID ASAP, and lots more. So let's get started.

TIPS

Wear a mask.

Rapidity of transmission is very high for Delta and Omicron, which is why in healthcare and crowded settings, it's very important to wear an N95 or FFP2 mask.

Clean your hands.

Make sure you wash your hands for about 20 seconds with soap and water. In the absence of soap and water, you can use an alcohol-based sanitiser. It is especially important to sanitize after going outdoors or touching communal items.

Maintain safe distance.

If you have to be around people, maintain a distance of 2 meters or 6 feet from others around you as much as possible. In general, any gathering of over ten people should be avoided or conducted virtually.

Get vaccinated.

Everyone 15 and older is eligible to get the vaccine against Covid-19. CoviShield and Covaxin are two effective vaccines available in India. More information available at the official Government of India web portal for COVID vaccine registration.

MYTHS vs FACTS

  • Alcohol-based sanitizers safe to use for everyone.

FACT:Alcohols in the sanitizers have not been shown to create any relevant health issues. Little alcohol is absorbed into the skin, and most products contain an emollient to reduce skin dryness.

  • It is better to use hand-sanitizers than wear gloves.

FACT:Wearing gloves risks transferring germs from one surface to another and contaminating your hands when removing them. Wearing gloves does not replace cleaning hands.

  • The Omicron variant is more contagious than previous variants.

FACT:Scientists say that Omicron is more contagious, but it doesn't appear to transmit in ways that are different from earlier versions of the virus. That means the same precaution rules apply but become more important, even if you are vaccinated or have been infected before. Symptoms of this variant also seem to be milder than those of previous ones.

  • Healthy, young adults cannot be affected by COVID.

MYTH:Older people and younger people can be infected by the COVID-19 virus, but the infection is usually more severe for old and very young people. Some people, on the other hand, experience very mild symptoms. WHO advises people of all ages to take steps to protect themselves from the virus, or example by following good hand hygiene and good respiratory hygiene.

  • COVID can be spread through 5G networks.

MYTH:Viruses cannot travel on radio waves/mobile networks. COVID-19 is spread through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes or speaks. People can also be infected by touching a contaminated surface and then their eyes, mouth or nose.
